# User Scenarios — IndoSupplier

## About This Document

This document describes realistic end-to-end journeys for each user type. These scenarios show **why** each feature exists and **how** they connect to each other in practice. Use these to validate that the platform's feature set covers real user needs.

---

## Scenario 1 — Foreign Buyer: First-Time Sourcing from Indonesia

**Who:** Maya, a procurement manager at a home goods brand in Australia. Her company wants to diversify away from Chinese furniture suppliers and explore Indonesian alternatives.

**Goal:** Find 3 credible furniture suppliers in Java with export experience, compare them, and initiate contact.

---

**Step 1 — Discovery**

Maya finds IndoSupplier through a Google search: "furniture supplier Indonesia export." She lands on the category page `/kategori/furnitur` in English mode (auto-detected from her browser).

At the top she sees 2 Auction Placement cards — large cards with gold borders and "Premium Verified" badges. She notes these but wants to explore the organic results first.

**Step 2 — AI Search**

She clicks the AI Search toggle and types: *"solid wood furniture manufacturer for export to Australia, medium production capacity."*

The AI generates keywords: solid wood furniture, teak furniture manufacturer, mahogany furniture, furniture export, wooden home furniture, Java furniture producer.

Maya removes "mahogany furniture" and runs the search. Results appear grouped by keyword.

**Step 3 — Filtering**

She applies filters:
- Verification: Level 2 and above
- Export experience: Yes
- Response Rate: Responsive (above 80%)

She now has 12 results. She sorts by highest rating.

**Step 4 — Evaluating Profiles**

She opens 5 profiles. On each she checks:
- Company type (prefers PT — larger operations)
- PKP status (her finance team requires VAT invoices)
- Certifications (looking for quality standards)
- Photos of facility and products
- Response rate and average response time
- Countries previously exported to (hoping for Western countries)
- Language: wants English-speaking suppliers

She finds 4 promising ones and clicks "Add to Compare" on each.

**Step 5 — Comparing**

The comparison page shows 4 suppliers side by side. She eliminates one because their MOQ is far above her initial order size. She has 3 finalists.

She clicks "Export as PDF" and sends it to her manager.

**Step 6 — Sending RFQ**

From the Compare page, she clicks "Send RFQ to All 3." She fills in the form:
- Product: solid teak dining table and chair set, 4-seater, natural finish
- Quantity: 200 sets for first order
- Delivery: within 3 months, Sydney, Australia
- Notes: needs COO document, accepts T/T payment

She submits. Three suppliers receive WhatsApp notifications simultaneously.

**Step 7 — Receiving Responses**

Within 48 hours:
- Supplier A: responds with price range and lead time, confirms they can provide COO
- Supplier B: marks "Processing," says detailed quote coming in 2 days
- Supplier C: declines — minimum run is 500 sets

Maya continues with A and B off-platform via WhatsApp.

## Scenario 2 — Local Buyer: Quick Sourcing for Recurring Needs

**Who:** Budi, purchasing manager at a Jakarta snack manufacturer. He needs to find backup plastic packaging suppliers to reduce dependency on his current single supplier.

**Goal:** Find 2–3 alternative suppliers in Java and get a quick ballpark quote.

---

**Step 1 — Standard Search**

Budi logs in (Trusted Buyer, score 62). He types "plastik kemasan makanan" in the search bar.

Filters: Province = Jawa Tengah + Jawa Timur, PKP only, Verified Level 2+.

**Step 2 — Quick Profile Scan**

He scrolls through results. He looks for: response rate above 70%, at least 3 reviews, photo of actual production line. He opens 4 profiles, bookmarks 3.

**Step 3 — Multi-Supplier RFQ**

He goes to Bookmarks, selects all 3, clicks "Send RFQ to Selected."

Form: PP food-grade plastic packaging, standing pouch with zipper, 150g, 50,000 units, within 6 weeks, BPOM-certified material required.

Done in under 5 minutes.

**Step 4 — Getting Responses**

All 3 respond within 24 hours (all marked "Very Fast"). Budi compares quotes in his RFQ inbox and contacts the most competitive one via WhatsApp.

## Scenario 3 — Foreign Buyer: Using Broadcast RFQ

**Who:** Lin, a sourcing agent in Singapore. She needs batik fabric suppliers for a Singaporean fashion brand and is not very familiar with the category.

**Goal:** Cast a wide net quickly, let interested suppliers come to her.

---

**Step 1 — Context**

Lin has a Premium Buyer score (87) — she uploaded her company registration and has a verified business email domain. She has used the platform for 6 months.

**Step 2 — Broadcast RFQ**

She goes to "Broadcast RFQ" → selects category: Tekstil & Kain.

She fills in:
- Title: "Batik fabric sourcing for fashion brand export to Singapore"
- Product: hand-stamped and printed batik fabric, cotton base, various motifs
- Quantity: starting 500 meters per motif, 5 motifs
- Timeline: sample in 4 weeks, bulk in 8 weeks
- Budget: SGD 8–15 per meter
- Notes: needs COO, prefers Yogyakarta or Solo region

She submits. The system confirms her score ≥ 80 and sends the broadcast to all active suppliers in the Tekstil & Kain category.

**Step 3 — Suppliers Respond**

12 suppliers receive a WhatsApp notification about the broadcast. Within 6 hours, 5 click "I'm Interested" in their dashboards.

Lin sees: "5 suppliers interested in your broadcast." She views their profile cards without the suppliers knowing she has looked.

**Step 4 — Selecting Suppliers**

Lin selects 3 whose profiles look most relevant (Yogyakarta-based, Verified, responsive). The broadcast converts into individual RFQ conversations with those 3.

The other 2 who expressed interest receive an automated message: "The buyer has already selected suppliers for this request."

## Scenario 4 — New Supplier: Registration and First RFQ

**Who:** Pak Hendra, owner of a CV in Surabaya that produces dried shrimp and spice mixes. A friend told him about IndoSupplier. He previously got a WhatsApp message from the IndoSupplier sales team inviting him to register.

**Goal:** Get listed and receive buyer inquiries.

---

**Step 1 — Registration**

Pak Hendra opens the registration link on his phone. He selects "I am a Supplier."

He enters: company name, business field, email, WhatsApp number. Gets an OTP, enters it. Creates his password.

The onboarding wizard starts. He fills in:
- Category: Agrikultur & Bahan Pangan → Bumbu & Rempah
- Location: Surabaya, Jawa Timur
- Company type: CV
- Tax status: Non-PKP
- Short description (in Bahasa Indonesia): "Produsen dan eksportir udang kering dan bumbu tradisional khas Jawa Timur sejak 2010."

Done in 8 minutes. His profile is live with a blue "Registered" badge.

**Step 2 — Completing the Profile**

The next day he logs in on his laptop:
- Uploads 6 photos: production area, packaged products, spice display
- Adds 3 product listings with MOQ
- Adds export destinations: Malaysia, Singapore
- Uploads his Halal MUI certificate for admin review

3 days later: "Sertifikat Halal MUI Anda telah diverifikasi." The Halal badge appears.

**Step 3 — First RFQ**

One week after registration, he gets a WhatsApp from IndoSupplier:

"Ada permintaan penawaran baru dari [Buyer dari Malaysia] [Trusted Buyer badge]. Produk: Udang kering grade A, 200kg, pengiriman dalam 3 minggu. Buka dashboard untuk lihat detail."

He opens his dashboard, reads the RFQ, clicks "Reply," enters price per kg, lead time, packing options. The buyer replies asking about custom packaging. They continue over WhatsApp.

**Step 4 — Upgrading to Level 2**

Pak Hendra notices Level 2 suppliers rank higher in search. He uploads his NIB document. Admin approves within 2 days. His badge turns green. Profile views increase noticeably over the following two weeks.

## Scenario 5 — Supplier: Winning an Auction Placement

**Who:** Ibu Sari, marketing manager at a textile manufacturer in Bandung. The company has been on IndoSupplier for 3 months, Level 3 Premium Verified. She wants to maximize visibility during trade season.

**Goal:** Win an Auction Placement slot on the Tekstil & Garmen category page.

---

**Step 1 — Discovering the Auction**

She opens her supplier dashboard → Advertising → Auction Placement.

She sees: "Open auction: Tekstil & Garmen — 3 slots, 14-day duration. Bidding closes in 4 days. Minimum bid: Rp 500,000."

**Step 2 — Placing a Bid**

She bids Rp 1,200,000 for a 14-day slot starting Monday. The system charges a refundable deposit.

She sees: "Your current ranking: 2nd of 6 bids."

**Step 3 — Monitoring**

Next day: "Your current ranking: 1st of 8 bids." A competitor has entered below her. She decides to hold her position.

**Step 4 — Winning**

The auction closes. WhatsApp: "Congratulations! You have won an Auction Placement slot for Tekstil & Garmen. Duration: 14 days. Confirm payment within 24 hours."

She confirms. The deposit is applied. The slot goes live Monday.

**Step 5 — Monitoring Performance**

Dashboard → Advertising → Active Ads:
- Day 3: 480 impressions, 34 profile clicks, 4 RFQs received mentioning they found her via search
- Her typical rate without the slot: 1–2 RFQs per week

She plans to bid again when this slot expires.

## Scenario 6 — Admin: Full Day of Operations

**Who:** The IndoSupplier admin team on a Tuesday.

**Morning (Content Admin):**
- Opens the verification queue: 7 new Level 2 requests overnight
- Reviews 5: approves 4 (documents clear), rejects 1 (blurry document — sends rejection: "Please re-upload a clearer photo of your NIB")
- Checks new supplier registrations: 9 new signups in the last 24 hours, 3 have very incomplete profiles
- Triggers manual WhatsApp follow-up from admin panel to those 3: "Hai! Profil Anda di IndoSupplier masih 20% lengkap. Tambahkan foto produk untuk menarik lebih banyak buyer."
- Reviews 2 Halal certification uploads → approves both (documents not expired)

**Late Morning (Ads Admin):**
- Reviews 3 new Regular Ad submissions: approves 2, requests revision on 1 (image too low resolution)
- Opens a new Auction session: Agrikultur category, 2 slots, 30 days, minimum bid Rp 800,000, 5-day bidding period
- Checks yesterday's ad revenue: Rp 8.4 million — notes in the weekly revenue tracker

**Afternoon (CS Admin):**
- Opens ticket queue: 11 open tickets
  - Resolves 4 (password reset, profile editing question, how-to for auction, upload error)
  - Escalates 1 to Tech Team (supplier profile not appearing in search — likely a bug)
  - Assigns 2 billing dispute tickets to Finance Admin
- Handles an abuse report: buyer reported a supplier displaying a Halal badge but their certificate appears expired. Moderator checks the cert upload date — confirmed expired 2 months ago. Suspends the Halal badge, notifies the supplier to upload a renewed certificate, closes the report with action taken.
- Responds to 3 live chat sessions (all resolved in-session)

**End of Day:**
- Checks analytics: 14 new supplier registrations today, 41 RFQs sent, top searched keyword: "supplier kemasan biodegradable" — flags to content team for an SEO article

## Scenario 7 — Sales Team: Onboarding a Supplier via Direct Outreach

**Who:** Rizky, a member of the IndoSupplier sales team. He is targeting food ingredient suppliers in Semarang.

**Goal:** Get 5 new suppliers registered from today's outreach session.

---

**Step 1 — Building the Contact List**

Rizky searches Google Maps for "supplier bahan pangan Semarang" and "distributor bumbu Semarang." He collects 20 business names and phone numbers from their public Google Maps listings.

**Step 2 — WhatsApp Outreach**

He sends a templated WhatsApp message to each:

*"Halo Pak/Bu, saya Rizky dari IndoSupplier — platform direktori supplier Indonesia untuk buyer lokal dan asing. Kami sedang ada buyer dari luar negeri yang aktif cari supplier produk pangan dari Jawa Tengah. Daftar gratis di [link] hanya 10 menit. Boleh saya bantu?"*

**Step 3 — Follow-Up**

Within 2 hours, 8 businesses reply. 4 say they are interested. Rizky sends them the direct registration link and offers to guide them through the process via WhatsApp call.

**Step 4 — Guiding Registration**

For one supplier who says "saya tidak biasa pakai aplikasi," Rizky joins a WhatsApp voice call and walks through each step of the registration wizard verbally while the supplier taps on their phone.

That supplier finishes registration in 12 minutes and has their profile live by end of the call.

**Step 5 — Follow-Up Next Day**

Rizky checks the admin panel: 3 of the 4 who registered have profiles that are less than 30% complete (no photos, no product listing). He sends personalized follow-up messages to each with specific tips ("Coba tambah foto produk dulu Pak, itu yang paling penting buat buyer asing").

By end of the week, all 3 have completed enough of their profiles to appear in search results.

## Scenario 8 — Buyer Uses AI Search Agent and Confirms Results

**Who:** Andre, operations manager at a food manufacturing company in West Java.

**Goal:** Find suitable coconut shell charcoal briquette suppliers for industrial heating with constraints on location, price, and responsiveness.

---

**Step 1 — Buyer Enters Natural Language Request**

Andre opens AI Search and types:

*"Bantu saya mencari arang briquettes untuk pembakaran, supplier di Jawa, harga kompetitif, bisa kirim rutin bulanan."*

He adds initial constraints:
- Preferred location: Java
- Budget: mid-range
- Certification preference: export-ready is a plus

**Step 2 — AI Extracts and Confirms Intent**

The AI shows extracted intent as editable chips:
- coconut shell charcoal briquette
- industrial combustion fuel
- regular monthly supply
- location: Java
- price: competitive

Andre removes one irrelevant chip and clicks **Run AI Search**.

**Step 3 — AI Agent Runs Autonomous Retrieval**

The AI agent executes internal search steps automatically:
- calls supplier search with expanded keywords (charcoal briquette, coconut charcoal, fuel briquette)
- filters by location and basic trust criteria
- checks profile quality and response rate
- estimates budget fit where price indicators exist

**Step 4 — Recommendation Page Appears**

Andre sees a dedicated recommendation page with:
- Best Match (top-ranked suppliers)
- Budget-Friendly Alternatives
- Backup Candidates

Each card includes a match score and a short explanation:
- "Strong product match + East Java + high response rate"
- "Good price fit but lower response speed"

**Step 5 — Buyer Confirms Before Action**

Andre selects 3 suppliers and clicks **Confirm Selection**.

No message is sent automatically until this confirmation step. After confirmation, Andre continues to Multi-Supplier RFQ flow from the selected list.

**Outcome**

Andre completes shortlisting in under 10 minutes with higher confidence because the agent explains why each supplier is recommended.
